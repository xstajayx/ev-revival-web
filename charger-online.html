Get Your Charger Online

You are not alone if this feels tricky. Many phones and tablets drop the charger hotspot during setup. Follow this guide step by step and keep reconnecting to the charger hotspot whenever your device switches away.

Before you start

  • Have your home Wi-Fi name (SSID) and Wi-Fi password ready before you begin.
  • Type your Wi-Fi password exactly as shown. It is case sensitive (capital and lowercase letters must match).
  • Keep this page open on a second device if possible, so you can follow each step.
  • Check your charger side label now. You will need the charger serial number and CBID.
  • The CBID is usually in this style: EU_EDR_CB12...

Open the charger hotspot (very important)

  1. Turn the charger off at the power supply, then turn it back on.
  2. Wait for startup to finish. The green and blue flashing lights must stop first (this can take up to 2 minutes).
  3. On your phone or tablet, open Wi-Fi settings.
  4. Find a Wi-Fi network that starts with 700 (this is your charger S/N).
  5. Connect to that network.
  6. When asked for a password, enter your charger CBID from the side label.
  7. If your device says No Internet or Connected without internet, stay connected anyway.
  8. You may see a Wi-Fi icon with an exclamation mark while connected to the charger hotspot. This is normal during setup.
  9. Modern phones often jump back to mobile data or home Wi-Fi automatically. Keep checking Wi-Fi settings and reconnect to the charger hotspot whenever this happens.

WLAN setup steps (inside charger page)

  1. Open the top-left menu.
  2. Select Network Interface.
  3. Select WLAN on the left.
  4. Set WLAN to Enabled.
  5. Enter your home Wi-Fi name (SSID).
  6. Enter your home Wi-Fi password exactly as shown (case sensitive).
  7. Set Security to WPA/WPA2 PSK.
  8. Set IP Settings to DHCP.
  9. Press Save.
  10. Go to Main Page.
  11. Set Pre-Sets to WLAN, then press Save again.

A) Vestel HS chargers

Login IP:

192.168.35.1

Username: admin

Password: printed in your supplied booklet (charger-specific). Example only: F4De*5/gf. Do not assume the example password will work on your charger.

  1. Check the side label and confirm your model includes HS.
  2. Connect your phone/tablet/laptop to the charger hotspot (network starts with 700).
  3. If your device warns no internet, ignore the warning and stay on the charger hotspot.
  4. Open your browser and go to 192.168.35.1.
  5. If the page will not load, first confirm you are still connected to the charger hotspot. Reconnect if needed.
  6. Log in with username admin and your own booklet password.
  7. Open menu → Network Interface → WLAN, and set WLAN to Enabled.
  8. Enter your home SSID and password exactly (password is case sensitive).
  9. Set Security = WPA/WPA2 PSK and IP Settings = DHCP, then press Save.
  10. Go to Main Page → set Pre-Sets = WLAN → press Save again.
  11. If your phone keeps switching networks, disable auto-join for other Wi-Fi networks temporarily and reconnect to the charger hotspot.

B) Vestel non-HS chargers

Login IP:

192.168.1.1

E.ON non-HS login: username admin / password eon01.

  1. Check the side label and confirm your model does not include HS.
  2. Connect to the charger hotspot (network starts with 700).
  3. If your phone says Connected without internet, stay connected and continue.
  4. Open browser and go to 192.168.1.1.
  5. If the login page does not appear, your phone likely switched away. Reconnect to the charger hotspot and try again.
  6. Log in with the correct non-HS credentials.
  7. Open menu → Network Interface → WLAN, and set WLAN to Enabled.
  8. Enter your home SSID and password exactly (password is case sensitive).
  9. Set Security = WPA/WPA2 PSK and IP Settings = DHCP, then press Save.
  10. Go to Main Page → set Pre-Sets = WLAN → press Save again.
  11. If your phone keeps switching networks, temporarily disable mobile data and auto-join to other Wi-Fi, then reconnect to the charger hotspot.

After saving

  • Allow up to 15 minutes for the charger to appear online.
  • When finished, close your browser and disconnect from the charger hotspot.
  • If it still does not come online, restart both the charger and your router, then repeat the steps.

Connectivity tips

  • The charger needs a stable home Wi-Fi connection.
  • Weak signal can cause dropouts and failed updates.
  • Garages, walls, and long distance from the router can reduce signal strength.
  • If needed, use a Wi-Fi extender or powerline adapter to improve coverage.
